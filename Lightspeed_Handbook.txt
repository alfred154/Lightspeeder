Scanning and Searching Products
1. The "Scanner First" Rule
To maintain 100% inventory accuracy, all products must be scanned using the barcode on the individual unit.
Procedure: Scan the barcode on the product. 
Verification: After scanning, look at the screen to ensure the exact product matches the physical item in your hand.
2. Using the Global Search Bar
If a barcode is damaged or missing, you must manually search for the item. Avoid "guessing" the item; use specific keywords to narrow the results.
Search by SKU: If you have the SKU number or barcode number from the shelf tag or price sticker, type it directly into the search bar. This is the most accurate manual method.
Search by Keyword: Type the Brand + Flavor + Nicotine Level.
Example: Type "Geek Bar Pulse Blue Razz 5%" rather than just "Geek Bar."
Avoid Generic Terms: Searching for "Disposable" or "Coil" will bring up thousands of items, increasing the risk of selecting the wrong product.

Quick Guide: Register Actions & Commands
To ensure our inventory and sales reporting remain accurate, 
please follow these strict guidelines regarding the "Action" menu in Lightspeed.
🚫 PROHIBITED ACTIONS
Do NOT use “Create Quote”: We do not use the quote function for standard retail. This can lock inventory and create "ghost" orders.

“Print Last Sale”: Use this if a customer requests a receipt after the transaction window has closed or if the printer jammed during the initial print.
“Open Cash Drawer”: Use this action to open the drawer electronically without a physical key.

Split Payments & Partial Loyalty Use

Split payments are simple and handled directly on the Pay screen.

To split a payment:
1. On the Pay screen, locate the total amount in the upper right
2. Tap to edit the total — enter the amount for the first payment (e.g. $20 cash)
3. Complete the first payment
4. The remaining balance will appear for the second payment (e.g. card, loyalty, etc.)

This also works for loyalty redemptions.

If a customer wants to use only part of their loyalty (e.g. $5):
1. Change the total to $5
2. Select Loyalty as the payment method
3. Pay the remainder with another method

Note:
No special steps are required—just edit the total and proceed as normal.

Refund vs Void

A void cancels a transaction as if it never happened, removing it from sales reports and returning items to inventory without reversing payments, whereas a return (refund) occurs after a sale is completed to formally process a reversal of funds and inventory. 

Voids are used for immediate corrections, like adding back Loyalty, while returns manage customer transactions post-sale.

Void to return Loyalty. Return to refund money to the customer. 

Returning Loyalty 

If you “spend” a customer’s loyalty by applying loyalty as the first of a partial payment, you will need to follow these steps. THIS WILL NOT REFUND MONEY, ONLY LOYALTY!
